Which one is right for you?

That depends on what you're looking for, not which app is objectively "better." If you value an established, ORCHA-certified app with quarterly questionnaires and expert-led content, Balance may already cover what you need. If a built-in view connecting trends and patterns across symptoms and triggers matters to you, that's an area where Ripple's Trigger Tracker and Biological Correlations differ from Balance today. Some people use both — a comparison table can't make that call for you, only lay out what's actually there.

Frequently asked questions

Which app should I use?

This page doesn't make that call. Balance and Ripple make different trade-offs, and the table above lays out what each currently offers so you can weigh what matters to you.

Do either of these apps connect to Apple Health, Garmin, or MyFitnessPal?

No. As of today, neither app integrates with those platforms. Both rely on manual entry.

Which app shows trends or patterns across symptoms?

Balance shows a 1-, 3-, and 6-month graph for each symptom individually. Ripple adds two features that connect symptoms and triggers to each other over time: the Trigger Tracker's correlation engine and the Biological Correlations chart.

Do both apps produce a report I can share with my doctor?

Yes. Balance generates a shareable clinician Health Report from your journal entries, with an editable symptom summary before you generate it. Ripple generates a printable GP brief through its Evidence Engine feature.

How much does each app cost?

Both offer a free tier and an optional paid subscription for additional features. Pricing varies by region and changes over time, so check each app's current listing directly rather than relying on this page.

Is Ripple endorsed by ORCHA, the NHS, or Apple?

No. Those recognitions belong to Balance. Ripple isn't endorsed by, recommended by, or affiliated with ORCHA, the NHS, Apple, Balance, or any other organisation referenced on this page.
